An undying ambition for a variety of skills.

I always put in bounds of effort in anything he is interested in deeply. Motivated to be the best version of myself possible, I put time into learning many different skills and practicing them often until I am satisfied with the outcome.

Being able to study programming is a dream come true for me as I enjoy problem solving a lot, and programming involves a lot of it. On top of that, I am able to practice these different skills and theories often, and only get better as I practice more and more.

Shown below are just a few of the different things that I have enjoyed practicing and mastering, whether it be purely for fun or with goals in mind as well.

Let him cook.

I've been pretty passionate about cooking ever since I was a kid. Learning the ins and outs of our kitchen at a young age, I was constantly experimenting with cooking different cuisines and practicing different techniques.

Becoming a Line Cook was a great experience for me since I got to learn what it looks like to master the art of a dish, and the process of constantly changing small things here and there in order to make something as perfect as possible.

One skill that I always loved to practice was knife skills and frying skills, as I always gravitated towards the things that would push me to my limits and keep me engaged.

Nowadays, I still cook often, and I am always looking for new recipes to try out and new techniques to learn.

Problem solver.

Gaming was always a great casual pass time for me as a child, but I noticed that there always felt like there was more than burning time to it than meets the eye. A competitor at heart, I enjoyed being the best at the games that I played.

Games are a big part of what makes me able to think in a logical manner quickly, since a large part of being a competitive gamer is learning and picking things up faster than the people around you in order to stay on top.

Being able to break things down into each component and process them quickly is important as you are able to learn things much faster, which is important no matter what you are trying to learn.

Even though games have now returned to a more casual pass time for me, the lessons I've learned from playing will never leave me.

Tinker in order to learn.

I've always been interested in how computers work as I have always found them to be quite abstract and fascinating. Knowing this, I took it upon myself to learn how to build a computer.

After building my first computer, I was hooked. I loved the process of building a computer and learning about the different components and how they work together in order to make a computer run.

I enjoyed being able to piece the parts together and watch as these different components worked and became a whole unit to create a machine capable of doing computations faster than man.

Shown here is a gallery of the different computers I have worked on over the past few years.

Head first, always.

Programming. Often seen as quite a daunting skill to learn, I was able to carry over my learning techniques built from gaming and apply them right into learning code.

Programming opened a new outlet for me to solve more practical problems in the world, and also gave me a sandbox to create things as long as I have the skills to do so. Boundless creative potential allows programming to be a great outlet for harnessing any skill learned.

Having always taken an interest into data in the past years, I decided that this career would give me many options, while also being something that I know I will be able to practice and enjoy from anywhere, no matter the location or time.

What separates a good programmer and an amazing programmer? Still not sure yet, but these are questions that I ask myself. I know that there are many different interesting things to learn, and I will continue to challenge myself to learn it all.
